Screen Printing

Silkscreen printing is a printing technique that can be done on different types of textile. This technique is very often used for prints with less than six colors. We first create a film, which will serve as a support for your logo, then we install the film on a silk frame to allow the paint to pass through the film and give shape to your design.

  • Maximum 6 colors.

    • Embroidery

    Embroidery is made from yarn and allows to have an unlimited number of colors. Embroidery allows to have a dense logo and some relief, it is one of the best options for durability compared to other techniques of printing on textile.

    How does it work? First of all, we have to create a matrix of your logo and then prepare the different color threads for the logo. (We keep the matrix in the folder so you only pay for it once). The unit price may vary depending on the size of the logo and the quantity of unit to be embroidered. We also offer the patch and embroidery-text service which allows to add names without having to create a matrix.

    Vinyl Transfer

    Vinyl transfer is applied to the garment using a heat transfer press. It is often used for single prints or for numbering and individual identification, perfect for sportswear. This technique makes it possible to make more specific placements and print on different materials.

    Sublimation

    Sublimation is a very interesting type of printing for the long term. This technique transfers the ink to the synthetic fabric at very high temperatures. After heating, the ink changes from solid to gaseous. This process allows incomparable durability and can be cleaned without any problem of washing away.

    Pad Printing

    Pad printing is mainly used on products or locations that are difficult to print through other printing processes. This technique allows printing on plastic, metal and glass. The print is produced by a silicone pad adapted to the surface that absorbs the logo to be engraved and then deposits it on the support.

    • LED Neon

    This product is perfect for creating vintage signs. The neon LED is attached to an acrylic support also called Plexiglass. It also offers the same rendering as gas neon but without the disadvantages: it does not emit heat, it consumes very little electricity and it is powered by low voltage. The price of a neon LED sign will depend on two elements; the size and total length of neon to use. There are two types of installation, wall and suspension.
